# InventoryVision AI - Business Model & Market Strategy

## Revenue Model

#### Starter Tier - $299/month
**Target**: Small single-location retailers (500-2,000 sq ft)
- Up to 4 cameras
- Basic 3D inventory tracking
- Daily reconciliation reports
- 30-day data retention
- Email support
- Core API access

#### Professional Tier - $799/month
**Target**: Medium retailers or small chains (2,000-5,000 sq ft)
- Up to 12 cameras
- Advanced analytics & insights
- Shrinkage detection alerts
- 90-day data retention
- Priority support
- Full API access
- Custom report builder
- Mobile app access

#### Enterprise Tier - Custom pricing
**Target**: Large retailers and multi-location chains
- Unlimited cameras
- Multi-location dashboard
- Predictive analytics & ML models
- Custom data retention
- Dedicated account manager
- White-label options
- Custom integrations
- On-premise deployment option
- SLA guarantees

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
1. **Traditional Inventory Management Software**
   - SAP, Oracle, NetSuite
   - *Our advantage*: No manual scanning, visual intelligence

2. **Smart Shelf Solutions**
   - RFID-based systems (Avery Dennison, Checkpoint Systems)
   - *Our advantage*: No per-item tags, uses existing cameras

3. **Computer Vision Startups**
   - AiFi, Trigo, Grabango (checkout-focused)
   - *Our advantage*: Inventory-focused, broader application

### Indirect Competitors
- Manual inventory processes
- Barcode scanners and handheld devices
- Physical stock audit services

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Zero Infrastructure Investment**: Use existing cameras
2. **3D Spatial Understanding**: More accurate than 2D systems
3. **VLM Intelligence**: Context-aware, not just object detection
4. **Faster ROI**: No hardware costs, immediate value
5. **Privacy-First**: Inventory-focused, not surveillance

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Early Adopters (Months 1-6)
**Target**: Tech-forward boutique retailers, specialty stores
- **Strategy**: Direct sales, pilot programs, case studies
- **Goal**: 20-50 customers, proof of concept
- **Channels**:
  - Direct outreach to innovation-minded retailers
  - Retail technology conferences (NRF, RetailTech)
  - Beta program with select partners

### Phase 2: Market Validation (Months 7-18)
**Target**: Small to medium retail chains (3-20 locations)
- **Strategy**: Expand sales team, develop partnerships
- **Goal**: 200-500 customers, establish market fit
- **Channels**:
  - POS system partnerships (Shopify, Square)
  - Retail consultant network
  - Industry trade shows
  - Digital marketing campaigns

### Phase 3: Scaling (Months 19-36)
**Target**: Enterprise retailers, franchises
- **Strategy**: Build enterprise sales, expand internationally
- **Goal**: 1,000-2,000 customers, market leader position
- **Channels**:
  - Enterprise sales team
  - System integrator partnerships
  - Reseller network
  - Industry analyst relations (Gartner, Forrester)

### Phase 4: Market Dominance (Year 3+)
**Target**: All retail segments, global expansion
- **Strategy**: Platform ecosystem, vertical expansion
- **Goal**: 5,000+ customers, industry standard
- **Channels**:
  - Global partner network
  - Camera manufacturer bundling
  - Private equity portfolio company rollouts

## Financial Projections

### Cost Structure

#### Fixed Costs
- **Engineering & Development**: 40% of expenses
- **Sales & Marketing**: 30% of expenses
- **Customer Success**: 15% of expenses
- **Operations & Admin**: 15% of expenses

#### Variable Costs
- **Cloud Computing**: ~$50-100 per customer/month
- **Support Costs**: ~$25 per customer/month
- **Payment Processing**: 2.9% of revenue

### Break-Even Analysis
- **Fixed Monthly Costs**: ~$500K (at scale)
- **Contribution Margin**: ~70% (after variable costs)
- **Break-Even Point**: ~1,000 customers at average $799/month
