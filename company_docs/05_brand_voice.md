## Brand Don'ts - Avoid These Patterns

### ‚ùå Hype Without Substance
Bad: "Revolutionary AI that will completely disrupt retail forever!"
Good: "Our computer vision platform reduces inventory errors by 60% on average, based on data from 200+ retailers."

### ‚ùå Fear Mongering
Bad: "If you don't adopt AI now, your business will fail!"
Good: "Early adopters of inventory automation see competitive advantages in accuracy and efficiency."

### ‚ùå Dismissing Existing Solutions
Bad: "Barcode scanners are obsolete and stupid."
Good: "While barcode scanners work well for POS, they require manual scanning for inventory. We automate that process."

### ‚ùå Overly Technical Without Context
Bad: "Our ViT-based VLM with SAM3D instance segmentation leverages multi-view geometry..."
Good: "We use advanced AI vision models (like GPT for images) combined with 3D reconstruction to automatically identify and track products."

## Crisis Communication Guidelines

### If System Outage Occurs
1. **Acknowledge immediately** (within 30 minutes)
2. **Be transparent** about cause and impact
3. **Provide timeline** for resolution
4. **Update regularly** every hour until resolved
5. **Post-mortem** share lessons learned

Template: "We're aware of [issue] affecting [systems]. Our team is actively working on resolution. Expected fix: [time]. We'll update every hour. We apologize for any disruption."

### If Privacy Concern Raised
1. **Take seriously** and investigate immediately
2. **Be transparent** about our practices
3. **Invite dialogue** and independent audits
4. **Reiterate commitment** to privacy-first principles

Template: "We take privacy concerns extremely seriously. [Specific response to concern]. Our system is designed to [privacy protection]. We welcome questions and independent audits."

## Content Approval Process

### Social Media Posts
- **Creator**: Drafts post following brand guidelines
- **Review**: Marketing lead reviews for brand voice
- **Approve**: Founder/CMO approves customer stories, product claims

### Blog Posts & Whitepapers
- **Draft**: Writer creates content
- **Technical Review**: Engineering reviews technical accuracy
- **Brand Review**: Marketing reviews voice and messaging
- **Legal Review**: Legal reviews claims and compliance (if needed)
- **Final Approval**: Head of Marketing

### Customer-Facing Claims
Any quantitative claims (ROI, accuracy %, time savings) must be:
1. Based on real customer data
2. Representative (not cherry-picked outliers)
3. Include "on average" or "up to" qualifiers when appropriate
4. Documented for verification
